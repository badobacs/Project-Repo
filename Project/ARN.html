Keeping carnivorous plants outdoors

As is known, many insect carnivorous plants can be kept outdoors in garden conditions, of course, which come from temperate belts. In the case of plants of tropical origin, one encounters compromises. Based on this, I started a multi-year experiment for him on how to keep Pygmy Sundews outdoors under controlled conditions. Pygmy Sundews are native to western Australia but, unlike species, occur in large numbers from the north-west coast to the city of Perth. I do not take into account the characteristics of the growing medium because, of course, there may be differences as species, I only focused on the weather characteristics. Last year, the test subject was only one specimen of Drosera roseana, until this year, individuals of 8 species were included in the experiment, including Drosera sargentii, which was sensitive to me.
